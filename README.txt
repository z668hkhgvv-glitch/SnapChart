SNAPCHART - BRANDYWINE BULLDOGS SIDELINE PLAY CHART
=========================================

A simple app for charting plays on the sideline and seeing what works.
It runs on your iPad, saves your data on the device, and works with no
internet once it's set up.


PUT IT ON YOUR IPAD (free, no App Store)
----------------------------------------
1. Put this whole folder online so it has a web address. Easiest free
   option: go to tiiny.host in Safari and upload play-chart-app.zip.
   (Netlify's uploader does not work well on iPads, so use tiiny.host.)
2. Open the link it gives you in SAFARI.
3. Tap the Share button (square with an up arrow), scroll down, and tap
   "Add to Home Screen", then "Add". Turn on "Open as web app" if asked.

MAKE IT WORK OFFLINE (do this once)
-----------------------------------
- Open the app FROM THE HOME-SCREEN ICON while you still have internet.
- Watch the badge under the title. When it turns green and says
  "Ready to use offline," it has saved itself.
- Close the app fully, then it will open in airplane mode.
- Needs iPadOS 16.4 or newer.


GAME MODES (you pick one when you tap "New game")
-------------------------------------------------
STANDARD - normal football. Quarter, Down, To-go, Run/Pass, plus
  Formation, Play call, Motion, Defensive front, and Coverage.
  A play is auto-marked effective on 1st down with 5+ yards, on 2nd
  down with half the yards to go, and on 3rd/4th when you convert.

7v7 - pass-only league. No Run/Pass, no quarter, no down/distance.
  Ball starts on the 40. You get 4 plays to reach the 20, then 4 to
  reach the 5, then 4 to score. The app shows the situation and moves
  the ball for you after each play. A play is effective if it reaches
  the line or gains its fair share for that down. Coverage only (no
  Defensive front).

SCRIMMAGE - 10 plays in a row, no downs, no turnovers. The app counts
  "Play 3 of 10" and starts the next series on its own. A play is
  effective if it gains 5+ yards. Has both Defensive front and Coverage.


ENTERING A PLAY
---------------
- Type yards as a normal number. Tap the green "+" next to it to flip
  to a red "-" for a loss. No minus key needed. It resets to "+" each play.
- The first letter of each word is capitalized for you.
- After you add a play, all the text boxes clear, so no backspacing.
- Formation, Play call, Motion, Front, and Coverage remember what you've
  typed before and suggest matches as you type.
- The Effective box checks itself by the rule above; tap to override.
- Tap any play in the log to fix it.


REPORTS (tap "Quick Report")
----------------------------
- Most Effective / Least Effective - every play, ranked.
- By Play Call - each call's success rate and average yards.
- By Hash - best and worst plays from the left, middle, and right.
- Best by Down - top plays on each down (not in scrimmage).
- By Series - how each 10-play series went (scrimmage only).
Tap any play in a report to jump back and fix it.


SUGGEST PLAY
------------
Tap "Suggest Play," set the situation, and it shows the calls that have
worked best in that spot THIS GAME. Tap "Use this play" to load it into
the chart, then just add the yards.


SAVING GAMES
------------
- "Save game" banks the current game without clearing it.
- "New game" saves the current game and starts a fresh one.
- "Season" shows every saved game, your season-long top plays, and a
  "Download season CSV" button for end-of-year review.
- "Export CSV" saves just the current game.
- "Export for Hudl" saves the game in Hudl's breakdown layout (see below).


MATCH YOUR DATA TO FILM IN HUDL
-------------------------------
The "Export for Hudl" button makes a spreadsheet built the way Hudl's
play breakdown wants it - one row per play, in order, with a Play #
column and Hudl-style headers (QTR, ODK, DN, DIST, HASH, YARD LN,
OFF FORM, PLAY TYPE, PLAY, MOTION, DEF FRONT, COVERAGE, GN/LS, EFF).

To put your charted data on the video:
1. Upload your game film to Hudl and let it break into one clip per
   play (Hudl Assist or your staff can do this).
2. Open that game's playlist in Hudl and choose Import Breakdown Data.
3. Load the file from "Export for Hudl." Each row drops onto its
   matching clip, so your calls and results are tied to the film and
   you can filter the video by them.

One thing to watch: the rows line up by order, so row 1 lands on
clip 1. If Hudl clipped a play you didn't chart (or the other way
around), glance down the list once and nudge it back in line.


TIPS
----
- Turn the iPad sideways for the widest view.
- Export a CSV now and then as a backup, since data lives on this iPad.
